# PrezAR

This was my university thesis for my bachelor degree at Eötvös Lóránd University in Budapest, Hungary.

<br />

It is common for people to point into the air while telling a story, hoping that the information to be conveyed can be absorbed as effectively as possible. As a solution to this, tools such as Prezi or PowerPoint have now become well-known. Their essence is to provide visual support. However, these solutions have their own limitations, namely that in large volumes they can require a long structure, a large projector, and in some cases they can distract too much from the presenter. In order to achieve an immersive presentation and storytelling, there is a huge emphasis on properly conveying emotions, which is difficult to achieve with a two-dimensional slide show.

As of the time of writing my thesis and developing this application, there were no such easy-to-use and accessible tool on the market or in the public consciousness that would allow you to place three-dimensional elements around the storyteller based on augmented reality instead of pointing them in the air. My goal was to create such an application. It can work on a smartphone or tablet with a more modern iOS operating system. All you have to do is edit a presentation with the **editing program** (my classmate so our thesis works would benefit from each other) and, using cloud technologies and storage, it can be played immediately, and can even be shared with a QR code for a screening. Our task is simply to hold the camera in front of the presenter and let him or her present, a presentation that is now much more integrated into our own physical reality, using our own reality as a basis and extending it.
